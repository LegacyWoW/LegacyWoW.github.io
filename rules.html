Guild War Rules*

Guild War is a unique, optional game mode that allows guilds to compete in the open world for control of key locations. Participation is not required, and Guild War does not directly impact players who choose not to opt in.

How to Participate: To join Guild War, a Guild Master must purchase a Guildhouse in any major city. Once established, guild members can speak with their assigned Guild Officer inside the Guildhouse to begin attacking, defending, or capturing locations.

Cities: Cities are the largest and most valuable locations in Guild War. They provide significant income and allow the controlling guild to set tax rates on player transactions, with all revenue deposited into the guild bank. To capture a contested city, a guild must acquire Marks of Control and deposit them into the guild bank under the tab corresponding to the target location. Once your guild reaches 50% control, the city becomes yours. Controlling a city allows you to assign guards, manage officers, and adjust tax rates. Cities require upkeep, and if control falls below 50%, the city becomes vulnerable to siege by other guilds. Cities can be sieged only once a day, only after the control falls below 50%. If it is successfully defended, it will be "locked" and unable to be attacked again for 3 days.

Forts: Forts are strategic military positions across the open world. Initially, they can be claimed using Marks of Control. Once captured, opposing guilds must weaken control by raiding the fort and defeating its Officer. After sufficient successful raids, the fort becomes contested again and may be captured by another guild. Forts require less upkeep than cities and are only vulnerable during designated raid windows. Forts can be attacked 5 times daily, but if successfully defended, the fort will not be able to be attacked again for 24 hours.

Outposts: Outposts are smaller locations chosen for their income potential or strategic positioning (such as Goldshire). They require minimal upkeep and can be weakened either by defeating the stationed Officer or through Marks of Control. Outposts can also expand into nearby areas, increasing your guild’s territorial reach. There is no capped amount of times these can be attacked, but if defended it will be protected from attacked for 12 hours.

Territories: Territories are adjacent lands influenced by nearby controlled locations. Over time, stable control will extend your influence into surrounding areas. If your control weakens, these areas may revert to neutral. Territories provide minor gold income and act as a buffer between major holdings.

Additional Rules: Guilds may form alliances, allowing them to work together against rival guilds. During a siege, you may reinforce another guild by submitting an in-game ticket specifying who you wish to support and how. Reinforcements are subject to associated costs.

Guilds may also be vassalized. This can occur if a guild loses all of its territories or both the Guild Master and officers are defeated in a duel. Vassalized guilds fall under the control or influence of another guild. Guild Masters are allowed up to 5 key officers.

Guilds receive NPC bots to assist with both offense and defense. These units can join your group, be stationed at controlled locations for defense, or deployed in attacks.

The current limit is 5 NPCs per 25 guild members. Exceeding this limit is considered a severe violation: the Guild Master will be banned, and the guild will be permanently disbanded. *Please note this is not a complete list, and may be added to or adjusted as needed.
